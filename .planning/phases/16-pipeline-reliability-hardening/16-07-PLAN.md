<objective>
Close the two operator-visibility gaps from the Phase 15 dogfood run:

- **16j — Verifiable operator notification.** Three gates fired during the ship (a 78-minute security gate, the merge-approval gate, the CLI-footgun retry) and the operator saw NONE of them — `notify_fired` logged `unexpected:false` each time while delivering nothing a human saw. Since ntfy/desktop-notify expose no delivery receipt (RESEARCH A3), the only genuinely verifiable signal is one DevFlow fully controls: a persistent, escalating pending-gate indicator in `devflow status` that survives until the operator acts. This does NOT touch `fire_gate_notify`'s exit-code checking (that stays a thin best-effort wrapper) — it ADDS a status-side banner. It MUST reuse `truncate_reason` when rendering the (possibly agent-controlled) gate context.
- **16h — Cross-attempt Ship/Code history view.** Reconstructing "what has been tried and fixed so far" currently requires hand-diffing `events.jsonl` and successive `REVIEW.md` snapshots. Derive a per-phase attempt timeline by correlating `events.jsonl` (schema v1, the read side) with 16b's retained `.devflow/history/phase-NN/` captures and any retained `REVIEW.md` — inventing no new store (canonical reference: "16h should derive from this, not invent a new store").

Depends on 16-03 (16b's retained-capture path scheme is what 16h correlates against) and 16-06 (shares main.rs — sequenced after it).
Purpose: an open gate is impossible to miss, and the loop's attempt history is one command away.
Output: a persistent/escalating status banner, a `history.rs` correlator, and a CLI surface for it — with tests.
</objective>
